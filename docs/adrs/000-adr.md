# ADR 000: Architectural Decision Record

## 📝 Context and Problem Statement

During our work on the Portefaix project, we will have to make architectural
decisions about the platform and associated tools & processes.

When making decisions, we should record them somewhere for future reference, to
help us remember why we made them, and to help teams working in related areas
understand why we made them.

## Considered Options

- [Michael Nygard's template](http://thinkrelevance.com/blog/2011/11/15/documenting-architecture-decisions) – The first incarnation of the term "ADR"
- [MADR](https://adr.github.io/madr/) 2.1.2 – The Markdown Architectural Decision Records
- [Sustainable Architectural Decisions](https://www.infoq.com/articles/sustainable-architectural-design-decisions) – The Y-Statements
- Other templates listed at <https://github.com/joelparkerhenderson/architecture_decision_record>
- Formless – No conventions for file format and structure
- Github issues

## 💡 Decision

Chosen option: "MADR 3.0.0", because

- Implicit assumptions should be made explicit.
  Design documentation is important to enable people understanding the decisions later on.
- The MADR format is lean and fits our development style.
- The MADR structure is comprehensible and facilitates usage & maintenance.
- The MADR project is vivid.
- Version 3 is the latest one available when starting to document ADRs.

## 📈 Consequences

This repo holds Architecture Decision Records. The approach is described by
Michael Nygard in [this article](http://thinkrelevance.com/blog/2011/11/15/documenting-architecture-decisions).
